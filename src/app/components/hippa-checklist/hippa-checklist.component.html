HIPAA Checklists
Under Welloculus, we have gone through HIPAA compliance and identified following checklists that is a collection of checks to be followed by all kind of entities associated with a healthcare project. HIPAA or Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act provides checklists under three main categories that should be kept in account while working on a healthcare project.

These categories are:
  1. Security
  2. Privacy
  3. Breach


There are certain safeguards under each one these categories. Checkpoints falling under these categories and their underlying safeguards are mentioned below:

Security Checklist

Administrative Safeguards

  1. Security Management Process: Policies and Procedures to prevent, detect, contain, and correct security violations
  2. Risk Analysis: Conduct a risk analysis of e-PHI systems to identify threats, vulnerabilities, impact of risk, likelihood of occurrence and existing risk mitigation controls
  3. Risk Management: Policies and Procedures to reduce risks and vulnerabilities to an appropriate level by prioritizing and implementing risk management controls
  4. Assigned Security Responsibility: Designate a Security Officer to develop and implement policies and procedures
  5. Workforce Security – Appropriate Access: Policies and Procedures to govern initial and ongoing access to PHI; reviews of access for continued appropriateness
  6. Workforce Security – Terminating Access: Policies and Procedures to terminate employee access to PHI
  7. Workforce Security – Security Awareness Training: Training all employees on security awareness upon hire and continually
  8. Incident Response Procedures: Designate appropriate individuals and procedures for security incidents, including response, mitigation, documentation and corrective actions
  9. Network Security – Malicious Software Protection: Policies and Procedures to protect against, identify and report malicious software
  10. Business Continuity: Policies, Procedures and Training to address interruptions to business activity
  11. Business Associate Contracts: Agreements with Business Associates to ensure proper identification of PHI controls

Physical Safeguards

  1. Facility Access Controls: Policies and Procedures to grant, monitor and terminate access to physical facilities
  2. Facility – Emergency Planning: Policies and Procedures to restore data in the event of emergency
  3. Facility Procedures – Unauthorized Access: Policies and Procedures for identifying and responding to unauthorized access to physical facilities
  4. Maintenance and Modification of Facility: Policies and Procedures to monitor and document repairs and changes to physical physicality that impact security
  5. Workstation Security: Policies and Procedures to control access to, changes to and use of employee workstations
  6. Device and Media Controls: Policies and Procedures to address use of, storage, transmission, reuse, tracking and destruction of removable media that store ePHI

Technical Safeguards

  1. Unique Name for User Identity: Policies and Procedures to assign unique identifiers for access to PHI to track usage
  2. Computer Session Inactivity: Policies and Procedures to terminate electronic sessions based on a predetermined time of inactivity
  3. Transmission of ePHI: Policies, Procedures, audit controls, hardware and software to encrypt, monitor and record the transmission of ePHI
Privacy Checklist
  1. PHI Uses and Disclosures for Treatment, Payment, or Operations: Patient authorization for the disclosure of PHI for treatment, payment and healthcare operations is implied
  2. PHI Uses and Disclosures that Require Authorization: Patient authorization is required to disclose psychotherapy notes, PHI for marketing, the sale of PHI; elements of valid authorization
  3. PHI Uses and Disclosures Requiring Notice and Opportunity to Object: Certain disclosures require prior patient notice and opportunity to object: disclosures to family, facility directories, emergencies, and others
  4. PHI Uses and Disclosures without authorization: Certain disclosures do not require prior patient notice: court orders, law enforcement investigations, public health activities and others
  5. PHI Uses and Disclosures- Fund Raising: Disclosure of patient PHI for fund raising purposes requires authorization in most cases
  6. PHI Uses and Disclosures- Research: Disclosure of patient PHI for research purposes requires authorization in most cases
  7. PHI Uses and Disclosures- Personal Representatives and Family: Disclosure of patient PHI to patient family members/representatives is generally permitted, with exceptions
  8. PHI Uses and Disclosures- After Death: Patient privacy rights to PHI disclosure continue 50 years after death
  9. PHI Uses and Disclosures- Identity Verification: Prior to PHI disclosure, entities must verify the identify and authority of the requesting party
  10. PHI Uses and Disclosures- De-identification: Privacy Rule restrictions related to PHI disclosures do not apply to de-identified information
  11. PHI Uses and Disclosures- Safeguards for PHI Communication: Entities should ensure that channels of communication are appropriate for transmission of PHI
  12. PHI Uses and Disclosures- Minimum Necessary Standard: PHI disclosures and requests should be limited to the minimum PHI necessary for the treatment, payment, or operational requirement
  13. Minimum Necessary Standard- Workforce Access: Employee access should be restricted to the minimum necessary, defined by entity policies
  14. Minimum Necessary Standard- Routine Disclosures: Entities should define the standard of “minimum necessary” for routine disclosures of PHI
  15. Minimum Necessary Standard- Non-Routine Disclosures: Entities should establish criteria for individual disclosures and review of such disclosures
  16. Patient Rights- Disclosure Restrictions: Patients may request certain restrictions on PHI disclosure and use; entity agreement to restrictions is not comprehensive
  17. Patient Rights- Alternative Communication: Patients may request alternative locations and means communication
  18. Patient Rights- Access to PHI: Patients may inspect and request copies of PHI
  19. Patient Rights- PHI Amendments: Patients may request amendments of health records
  20. Patient Rights- Accounting of Disclosures: Patients may request an account of disclosures of PHI made by the entity
  21. Notice of Privacy Practices- Availability: Covered entities must provide the Notice to patients, in physical locations and on websites
  22. Notice of Privacy Practices- Acknowledgment: Covered entities must make good faith efforts to obtain patient acknowledgement of the Notice
  23. Business Associates: Covered entities may disclose PHI to business associates if the business associate provides safeguards PHI (contracts, monitoring, enforcement)
  24. Privacy Officer: Covered entities must designate a privacy officer to develop and implement policies and procedures
  25. Contact Officer: Covered entities must designate a contact person to receive complaints and provide information regarding the Notice of Privacy Practices
  26. Employee Training: Covered entities must provide adequate training to employees on Privacy Rule policies and procedures
  27. Complaints: Covered entities must provide opportunities for and responses to complaints; complaints and resolution must be documented
  28. Mitigation of Improper Disclosures: Entities must take steps to mitigate the effects of improper PHI disclosure
  29. Sanctions: Covered entities must sanction employees for violations of Privacy policies and procedures
  30. Record Retention: Documents must be retained according to the period of retention required by law
  31. Non-retaliation: Entities must refrain from retaliating patients who exercise HIPAA rights
  32. Non-waiver: Entities may not condition treatment of patients upon the patient’s waiver of HIPAA rights
  33. Corrective Actions: Entities must correct violations within 30 days of occurrence
Breach Checklist
  1. Identifying Breach: Covered Entities and Business Associates must identify whether an unauthorized PHI disclosure has occurred; certain exceptions apply
  2. Patient Notice: Covered Entities and Business Associates must notify patients of an unauthorized PHI disclosure in the required time frame, with the required content, and by the required method(s)
  3. HHS Notice: Covered Entities and Business Associates must notify HHS of unauthorized PHI disclosures within the required time frame (greater or less than 500 patients impacted)
  4. Media Notice: Covered Entities and Business Associates must notify the media of unauthorized PHI disclosures within the required time frame if more than 500 patients have been affected
  5. Business Associate Notice: Covered Entities must notify Business Associates of unauthorized PHI disclosures within the required time frame
  6. Law Enforcement Delays: Covered Entities and Business Associates may delay notification to required parties of unauthorized PHI disclosure if law enforcement states that notification would impede a criminal investigation or damage national security; time frame of delay based on written and oral requests by law enforcement
  7. Investigation: Covered Entities and Business Associates must investigate unauthorized disclosures
  8. Mitigation: Covered Entities and Business Associates limit the damaging effects of unauthorized disclosures
  9. Contact Person: Covered Entities and Business Associates must designate an individual for patients, media, business associates and HHS to contact regarding unauthorized disclosures
